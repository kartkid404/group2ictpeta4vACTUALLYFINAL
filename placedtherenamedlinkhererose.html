Your Partner in Fighting Drug Abuse

Introduction: A Home Away From Home

Substance abuse is a significant issue, particularly among students, affecting their health, relationships, and academic performance. Schools play a crucial role in combating this problem by providing education, prevention, and early intervention in a supportive environment.

To address this, the Department of Education has strengthened its efforts by implementing Barkada Kontra Droga (BKD). This program educates students about the dangers of drug use and promotes a drug-free lifestyle, encouraging students to be advocates for change. Through BKD, schools and communities work together to create a safer, healthier student environment.

What is Barkada Kontra Droga?

Barkada Kontra Droga is a peer support group aimed at:

  • Promoting awareness of the dangers of drug abuse.
  • Organizing school-based intervention programs.
  • Collaborating with communities and local government units for initiatives with shared goals.

This group is supported by various stakeholders, including students, teachers, school administrators, barangay councils, local government units (LGUs), private institutions, and other stakeholders.

Program Objectives

  • Establishing a Barkada Kontra Droga movement.
  • Training individuals in advocating a healthy and productive drug-free lifestyle.
  • Creating a fully drug-free environment in homes, schools, workplaces, and communities.
  • Encouraging the productive use of time through alternative activities.
  • Providing support services for adopting the Barkada concept among families, educators, professionals, workforces, peer groups, and other sectors.

To promote people empowerment in leading a healthy lifestyle that is drug-free and productive.
